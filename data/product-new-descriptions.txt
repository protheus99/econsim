FieldName,Description
MainCategory,Top-level classification grouping similar types of products (e.g., Grocery Electronics Auto & Home Goods).
Subcategory,Secondary classification organizing products within a main category by function or usage (e.g., Fresh Food Appliances Automotive).
Product,Specific item being modeled and sold in the simulation.
purchaseFrequency_perHour_per1000Pop,Average number of times the product is purchased per hour per 1000 people. Serves as the base demand rate before modifiers.
publicDemand,Overall popularity or market interest level for the product on a 0 to 1 scale. Represents general consumer appeal.
publicNecessity,How essential the product is for basic living or survival on a 0 to 1 scale. Higher values mean demand remains strong during downturns.
publicLuxury,Degree to which the product is discretionary or status-driven on a 0 to 1 scale. Higher values indicate income-sensitive demand.
priceConcern,How sensitive consumers are to price changes for this product on a 0 to 1 scale. Higher values mean demand drops quickly when price increases.
qualityConcern,How much product quality influences purchasing decisions on a 0 to 1 scale. Higher values mean quality strongly impacts demand.
reputationConcern,How important brand reputation or seller credibility is when purchasing on a 0 to 1 scale. Higher values give strong advantage to trusted brands.
standardPrice,Baseline retail price used as the reference point for price elasticity modeling.
standardCost,Baseline production or wholesale cost used for margin and profit calculations.
exampleHourlySales_cityPop_2500000,Estimated number of units sold per hour in a city with 2500000 people based on base frequency and demand values.
